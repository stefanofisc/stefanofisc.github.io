About Me

Stefano is a Machine Learning Engineer at Cyberneid, where he develops and optimize Computer Vision models for real-world applications.
He is a PhD candidate in the International PhD Programme UNESCO Chair on Environment, Resource and Sustainable Development at Parthenope University of Naples, working in the Department of Science and Technology. His doctoral thesis focused on the development of Machine Learning algorithms for exoplanet detection in Kepler and TESS data. He also served as principal observer in different projects aimed at supporting the ESA's Comet Interceptor mission, performing observations at ground-based telescopes: Asiago Copernico and Telescopio Nazionale Galileo.
He obtained his master's degree in Machine Learning and Big Data at Parthenope University of Naples, Italy (July 2022).
As he is very keen in improving his communication skills (e.g. giving talks, effective writing), he attended several courses and schools mainly held by Nature Masterclasses

Research

During my PhD, I developed deep learning and diffusion-based models to identify planetary signals in stellar light curves, with peer-reviewed publications in high-impact journals.
I have collaborated with international teams, including NASA’s TESS Collaboration, Planet Patrol, and the Planetary Systems group at the Laboratoire d’Astrophysique de Marseille, where I spent a three months research stay.
I will soon contribute to the ESA/PLATO mission within the Core Science (PSM) Exoplanet Working Group.

(ii) Comets observation
As part of the research group supporting ESA’s Comet Interceptor mission, I conducted photometric observations of comets using the Copernico Telescope (Asiago, Italy) and the Telescopio Nazionale Galileo (La Palma, Canary Islands), contributing to data reduction and analysis.
